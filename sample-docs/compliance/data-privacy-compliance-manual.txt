Section: Data inventory and processing purpose

Purpose:
This page defines mandatory controls for privacy compliance. The control owner must maintain
current evidence, verify that responsibilities are assigned, and confirm that exceptions are reviewed before
the reporting period closes.

Policy Requirements:
1. The accountable team must document the business process, systems involved, data categories, and approval
   path for this control area.
2. Evidence must include timestamps, approver identity, control result, remediation status, and a link to the
   source record. Screenshots alone are insufficient unless supported by system-generated reports.
3. Exceptions must be classified as low, medium, high, or critical based on customer impact, regulatory impact,
   financial reporting impact, and operational resilience impact.
4. Critical exceptions require executive acknowledgement and a remediation plan with target dates, interim
   compensating controls, and validation criteria.

Operating Procedure:
The control performer reviews the population, selects the required sample, validates each item against the
policy criteria, and records pass or fail status. A reviewer independent from the performer approves the result.
When testing fails, the issue owner opens a remediation ticket and links it to the compliance evidence package.

Search Keywords:
compliance, audit evidence, remediation, risk rating, exception management, approval workflow, Data inventory and processing purpose,
Consent management and lawful basis, control owner, policy requirement, regulatory obligation, monitoring, certification.

Example Scenario:
During a quarterly compliance review, an auditor requests proof that data inventory and processing purpose was performed for all
in-scope applications. The compliance analyst exports the system record, verifies that each approval was
completed on time, and attaches the evidence to the audit workspace. One missing approval is classified as a
medium-risk exception because the control was completed late but before external reporting.
